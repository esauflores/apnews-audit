FE413 · web performance audit

apnews.com is shipping a slow newsroom.

Eight representative pages. Every single one fails the Lighthouse mobile performance threshold. Main-thread blocking time is dominated by third-party scripts the newsroom does not own or need on first paint.

baseline · the numbers

22–46 s for the largest story on the page to render.

22–46 s LCP (Largest Contentful Paint)
5–9 s TBT (Total Blocking Time)
9.1 MB page weight on first load
0.8 CLS on /donate (page-level)

Google's own threshold for a "good" LCP is 2.5 s. Every measured page is 9–18× over. TBT of even 0.6 s makes scrolling visibly janky on mid-range Android.

root cause · who is on the main thread

The newsroom does not own the first paint.

bootup-time · top scripts on first load (homepage)
apnews.com (first-party control bundle)3 704 mstotal — attributable to third parties: webcontentassessor.com (content quality script)1 870 msscript permutive.com (audience platform)1 764 mstotal dianomi.com (ad context)1 692 msscript googletag / GAM (header bidding)1 407 msscript cookielaw.org / OneTrust (consent SDK)1 258 msscript kameleoon.io (A/B testing)1 182 mstotal n.tv (native ad network)1 126 mstotal

Every one of those scripts runs before the headline image paints. None of them is required to render a news article. Together they push TBT past the threshold where scrolling becomes unusable on entry-level Android.
